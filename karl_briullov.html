KARL BRIULLOV

The Briullo family had seven children: brothers Fedor, Alexander, Karl, Ivan (died at the age of 20), Pavel (died in childhood) and sisters Maria and Yulia. Their father, Pavel Ivanovich Briullo, was a descendant of French protestants Brulelau who fled to Germany and there changed their French last name onto German: Brullo, which in Russian was transformed into Брюлло, and is transliterated as Briullo. Pavel Briullo was a professor of ornamental arts at the St. Petersburg Royal Academy of Arts. Alexander Briullo was born in 1798, and Karl a year later, in 1799. The brothers had been admitted to the Academy when they turned ten years, Alexander in 1809 and Karl - in 1810. In 1822, the brothers graduated from the Academy with honours. Upon graduation, and under the condition that an artist was not married, it was common then to send Russian artists for the internship abroad. In 1822, Academy sends both brothers to Italy for three years, with an annual stipend of five thousand roubles provided by the Society for the Encouragement of Artists, which was established in 1820. To get this internship, however, an artist had to be a Russian, and that’s how Alexander and Karl had become the only family members of the Briullo family whose foreign last name was changed into the Russian one by adding “v” at the end. In 1823, the brothers reached Rome. When they got there, they found there a whole colony of Russian artists, and a warm atmosphere in the house of a Russian diplomat Grigory Gagarin, under whose aegis they gathered along with other famous Russians.
