The majority of writings on Pompeii and Herculaneum can be placed under the umbrella term "travel literature." These include works both published -- such as travel guides -- and unpublished, for example, travel diaries of Russian nobles on a Grand Tour. This literature has recently been in the focus of the scholars' interest. Nineteenth century poets had been exploring the imaginative powers of the Vesuvian history as well.
